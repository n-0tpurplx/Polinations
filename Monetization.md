# Monetization & Development Policy

## Overview
This project is designed as a **free-to-play, non-pay-to-win, open political strategy simulation game**. The core philosophy is that gameplay success must always be determined by player decisions, coordination, and strategy — never by payment.

Monetization exists only to support infrastructure costs and development continuity, not to create competitive advantages.

---

## Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

The following rules apply at all times, regardless of player count, server cost, or development pressure:

- No pay-to-win mechanics  
- No paid gameplay advantages in combat, economy, politics, or diplomacy  
- No paid influence over visibility in global or regional systems  
- No paid acceleration of strategic actions (wars, production, movement, etc.)  
- No exclusive content that affects competitive balance  
- Free players must always remain fully competitive at the highest level  

If a feature violates these principles, it will not be implemented.

---

## Current Monetization Model (Phase 0)

### Citizen+ (Supporter Tier)
Citizen+ is a voluntary supporter role intended to fund development and server costs.

Allowed benefits:
- Cosmetic profile customization (themes, banners, layouts)
- Badge or supporter status display
- Expanded nation profile customization options
- Extra statistical history or analytics views (non-competitive)
- Quality-of-life UI enhancements

Not allowed:
- Gameplay advantages
- Economic boosts
- Combat bonuses
- Political influence advantages
- Increased visibility in global systems

---

### Donations
- Fully optional
- No in-game advantage
- Purely supportive

---

## Emergency Monetization Policy (Phased Expansion)

If the game experiences rapid growth or infrastructure costs exceed sustainable levels, additional monetization phases may be introduced under strict rules.

### Phase 1: Scaling Support (QoL Expansion)
May include:
- Extra saved configurations (loadouts, presets)
- Expanded historical logs and analytics
- UI customization options
- Additional automation/scheduling convenience tools

Rules:
- Must remain non-competitive
- Must not affect gameplay outcomes
- Must not affect visibility, power, or progression

---

### Phase 2: Infrastructure Optimization (Last Resort)
Only considered if scaling costs become critical.

May include:
- Backend performance prioritization (non-gameplay affecting)
- Advanced account management tools for large nations/alliances
- Server resource distribution improvements

Rules:
- Must not create competitive advantage
- Must not alter gameplay balance
- Must be purely technical or infrastructural

---

## Explicit Forbidden Monetization (Forever)

These will never be implemented:

- Pay-to-win mechanics of any kind  
- Paid resource generation or economic boosts  
- Paid military strength or combat effectiveness  
- Paid war advantages or outcomes  
- Paid political influence or voting power  
- Paid visibility manipulation in global feeds or systems  
- Loot boxes or gambling mechanics affecting gameplay  

---

## Design Philosophy

The game is built on the idea that:

> Strategy, coordination, and intelligence determine success — not money or waiting time.

Monetization exists to sustain the platform, not to shape the outcome of the game world.

---

## Enforcement

These rules are part of the core design contract of the project. Any future feature proposal must be evaluated against this document before implementation.

Deviations require a full revision of this policy and explicit transparency to players.

---

## Why?

These rules exist because the developer is strongly opposed to pay-to-win mechanics, which have become increasingly common in modern games.

The goal of this project is to ensure that success is always determined by player strategy, coordination, and decision-making — not financial advantage.

At the time of writing, the game operates on a free-tier model with optional supporter monetization. This may evolve in the future strictly under the constraints defined in this document.

---

## If these rules are broken

If you believe a feature violates the no pay-to-win principles, please report it to the development team.

All feedback will be reviewed carefully. The goal is to maintain a fair and competitive environment where free players remain fully competitive.
